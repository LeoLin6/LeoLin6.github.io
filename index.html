About me

Last updated: July 2026

I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Illinois (UIUC). My interests include robot learning, computer vision, dexterous manipulation, and software engineering. My goal is to build robots that can work reliably in the messy real world. I'm currently a robotics software engineer co-op at Amazon Robotics (Innovation Lab).

Last summer, I interned at K-Scale Labs, training humanoid robots for manipulation with VLMs and action diffusion models, and writing Rust firmware for VLA data collection. Before that, I worked on robot calibration software at Robnux, and did research on robot arms, teleoperation, and computer vision through NSF REU and labs at UIUC.

At UIUC, I was a co-chair for SIGRobotics, a special interest group under ACM@UIUC, where I led the embodied AI hackathon team and humanoid project team. I'm a 3x robot hackathon winner, including 1st place locally at the LeRobot global hackathon.

Feel free to contact me via email (leolin3 at illinois dot edu) or through any of the following links: LinkedIn, GitHub, Resume.

Past work

  • Led development of CRAFT — a low-cost, open-source tendon-driven dexterous hand with hybrid hard-soft compliance. The hand covers 33/33 Feix grasps, costs under $600, and was accepted to RSS 2026.
  • Built a sock-sorting robot for the LeRobot global hackathon (1st place local), using Gemini for high-level planning and training VLA + ACT models with LeRobot's training stack.
  • Trained humanoid policies and wrote robotics infrastructure at K-Scale Labs; earlier research includes a silent audio-dataset turret, Sawyer bottle handover with MoveIt, and LEAP-hand teleoperation with MediaPipe.
